What is Journalism?

Journalism is the job of finding information, checking if it’s true, and sharing it with the public. It helps people understand what’s happening in the world so they can make smart decisions.

Being correct Being fair Thinking for yourself Helping the community

How Journalism Changed Over Time

1600s

Some of the first printed newspapers started appearing in Europe.

1900s

Radio and TV let people hear and watch news right in their homes.

2000s

The internet and social media changed how news is made and how people get it.

Types of Journalists

Click a card to learn more about each type of journalist.

Investigative Journalist

Looks into hidden problems, big issues, and things people try to cover up.

News Reporter

Reports on breaking news and everyday events, usually very quickly.

Feature Writer

Writes longer stories about people and their experiences.

Photojournalist

Tells stories through strong and meaningful photos.

Broadcast Journalist

Shares news on TV or radio and speaks clearly to an audience.

Data Journalist

Uses charts, numbers, and patterns to explain what’s going on.
